While traditional computers use "bits" (0s and 1s), quantum computers use "qubits" which can exist in multiple states simultaneously—a phenomenon known as **superposition**. This allows them to process massive amounts of data in parallel, solving problems that would take today's fastest supercomputers thousands of years to complete.

But this isn't just about faster calculations—it's about a complete paradigm shift in computation.

**What Quantum Solves:**
1. **New Materials**: Simulating at the molecular and atomic level to create more efficient batteries or better solar panels.
2. **Post-Quantum Cryptography**: Developing new encryption standards that can withstand the power of quantum attacks.
3. **Logistics**: Optimizing global supply chains for maximum efficiency and minimum waste.
